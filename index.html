BHAGWAD GITA-THE ANSWER TO ALL QUESTIONS

We all want motivation in life, whenever we feel down we need something that triggers our emotions and gives us the motivation to do our work or take action towards our goals. That's why to get real-time motivation or clarity in life we should understand the life lessons from Bhagavad Gita, which is considered the greatest scripture of all time. Bhagavad Gita is not just scripture written by a saint, it is actually the real conversation between Lord Krishna and his best friend or disciple Prince Arjun. On the battlefield when Arjun got demotivated, he was not ready to do war, he was unhappy about his decision of war against family members, at that time Lord Krishna told the Bhagavad Gita to Arjun. Bhagavad Gita gave in-depth clarity to arjuna about his purpose in life, self-realization, what is right/ wrong as per Dharma, who we are, and what is karma, etc. Because of which he performed his duty under the guidance of the supreme god Lord Krishna without any demotivation. It's difficult to cover all the lessons of Bhagavad Gita in one article. But I will try my best to give you 5 important Life lessons from Bhagavad Gita in this article, that will give you more clarity as well as motivation in life to find your true purpose and achieve your goals. So let's start...

  1. Bad situations come by not doing anything, but to create a good situation, you have to take action -

    2. As per Bhagavad Gita, if you want to achieve something in life but instead of taking action towards it, if you put that responsibility on God or on your luck, you think that God should save me in every situation and help me achieve my goals without any effort then with this type of thought process you will always get the worst results and failures. It's just like you don't have to do anything to be dirty, just sit and you will be dirty but to get clean you have to take action, you have to go to the bathroom and take a shower. This is because our body is constantly fighting with the disorders of the world. And the person who becomes inactive in this fight keeps on losing in every aspect of his life. You only win in life or conquer any challenge in life if you are ready to take action and ready to give your 100% effort in that process. To convert all bad situations of life into good situations, you must have to take action. So start taking action towards your goals now. This is one of the most important lessons of the Bhagavad Gita.

  2. Change is a universal law, that's why every happiness or sorrow is temporary -

    3. Change is the only constant in life. Everything in this world is continuously changing, every minute of every hour of every day, change is always occurring, whether you like it or not. So the best way to handle changes is to not run away from them. You should face it, prepare yourself for that change and make the best of it. In Bhagavad Gita, Lord Krishna advises Arjun that-

    "Your senses create sensations like cold, heat, happiness, sadness or stress in you by coming in contact with the things that are heard, smelled, tasted or felt. These feelings are temporary. It goes away as it comes. So Arjun you learn to tolerate these changes."

  3. Keep your focus on your duty, not on the end result-

    4. This lesson talks about goal setting, like how most people decide what they want to achieve in life. But still, they are never able to cross the obstacles or distractions that come in their way of reaching the goal. There was a great story from Mahabharata which emphasizes this particular lesson. In the Mahabharata, Dronacharya, who used to be Arjuna's guru, when he is teaching archery to all his students, tells him that he has to aim at the eye of a wooden bird placed on a tree. Then he asks everyone what he is seeing on the tree, at that time someone says that he is able to see the bird sitting on the tree, then someone tells him the whole tree, the bird sitting on it, its wings, its eyes, everything is visible. But when Guru Dronacharya asked Arjuna, Arjun said that "I can see only the bird's eye and nothing else". Just like this, we will find many obstacles and distractions in the way of our goal which will divert our focus towards unimportant things. That's why you have to put your attention in your work with full enthusiasm that you do not see anything else. Whatever work you are doing right now just focus on the process and give your 100% in that and then leave the responsibility of result on god. So, among the 10 powerful lessons of Bhagavad Gita, this lesson highlights the importance of focusing on the process of achieving any goal in life.

  4. You become what you believe in yourself -

    5. Belief is the world's most powerful thing or we can say a feeling. Because it can heal a sick person, make a sad person happy, and can make a failed person progress. With belief, we can achieve anything in life. Every great person first convinces his mind that he is worth doing something big, he can do something big in his life. Only then does he have the courage to actually do that thing and once he has that courage he can achieve all his goals.

  5. Cowardice and fear are the enemies of success -

    6. The 5th lesson of Bhagavad Gita focuses on two enemies of success ie. cowardice and fear. Because fear is one of our oldest feelings that make us doubt our ability and this feeling comes out when we do not fully understand the situation either because of lack of knowledge. Or due to the inability to manage our emotions. This fear makes us cowards and deviates us from our goals. So you should pursue more knowledge and wisdom as these are the two things that will teach you how to handle unknown and unexpected situations in life.

    Knowledge and Wisdom destroy fear completely.
